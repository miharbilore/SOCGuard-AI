# SOCGuard AI: Blue Team Mock Defender

## 1. Overview
The **Blue Team Mock Defender** is a research-oriented component of the **Adversarial Agent Lab** (V3). Its role is to analyze adversarial candidates generated by the Red Team and propose high-precision, deterministic detection signatures.

## 2. Deterministic Mock Nature
During the initial prototype phase, the Blue Team uses a **Strategy Mapping** system. This ensures that defense proposals are:
*   **Predictable**: Proposes consistent rules for specific attack vectors.
*   **Safe**: Generates conservative patterns that prioritize avoiding false positives.
*   **Reviewable**: Every proposal is formatted as a `BlueTeamProposal` record for human evaluation.

## 3. "NEEDS_REVIEW" Only
Crucially, the Blue Team **never** mutates the production detection rules directly. All outputs are generated with a `NEEDS_REVIEW` status. They are intended for the **Adversarial Lab Records** and cannot be promoted to active rules without explicit human analyst approval and the versioned Rule Pack release process (V2).

## 4. False Positive (FP) Awareness
A key responsibility of the Blue Team is identifying potential FP risks. For every proposed rule, the module:
1.  Identifies common benign scenarios where the rule might trigger (e.g., educational content, code snippets).
2.  Suggests "Hard Negative" test cases to be used during the validation phase.

## 5. Defense Strategies
The module implements specific logic for diverse attack types:
*   **Direct Overrides**: Semantic matching for instruction-discarding phrases in multiple languages.
*   **Jailbreak**: Pattern matching for roleplay-induced safety bypasses.
*   **Fragmented Payloads**: Signal correlation for variable-based obfuscation.
*   **Syntax Escaping**: Structural analysis of JSON/Markdown boundary breaches.
*   **Indirect RAG**: Identification of hijacking attempts embedded within document markers.

## 6. Future Work: LLM-Assisted Proposals
Future iterations may use LLMs to analyze complex, novel Red Team variants and synthesize highly specific regex or keyword signatures. However, the governance model remains identical: LLMs may only **propose** defenses; the final authority and activation are reserved for the **Human Reviewer**.
